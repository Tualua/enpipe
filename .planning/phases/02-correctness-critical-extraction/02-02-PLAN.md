<objective>
Extract the two correctness-critical arithmetic pieces from `pipeline.py` into pure, directly unit-testable functions with ZERO behavior change (DEBT-02): `compute_chunk_seek_trim(table, s, e)` (the per-scene seek/trim math, D-04) co-located in `encoding/keyframes.py`, and `contiguous_run(next_append, ready)` (the high-water-mark flush ordering, D-05) module-level in `encoding/pipeline.py`. The file I/O (copyfileobj/unlink/next_append advance) stays in `pipeline.py` as a thin shell.

Purpose: PITFALLS.md flags these two as the highest silent-corruption risk in the codebase (a shifted seek/trim or a mis-ordered concat silently corrupts output). Making them pure functions gives them their first direct unit tests over synthetic edge cases, without touching a byte of encoded output. A mocked `run_encode` integration test then proves the wiring is byte-identical WITHOUT hardware.
Output: two pure functions, rewired call sites in `pipeline.py`, unit tests covering frame-0/on/off-keyframe boundaries and out-of-order chunk completion, a non-hardware mocked `run_encode` wiring test, and a re-run of the Phase-1 encode-parity gate proving the extraction left `movie.obu` byte-identical.
